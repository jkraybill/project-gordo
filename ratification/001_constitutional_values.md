## Summary of Content

Seven non-negotiable values that every Project Gordo downstream must uphold:

1. **Consent Is Mutual** -- all substantive decisions require explicit agreement of all affected parties
2. **Collaborator Dignity Is Unconditional** -- agency, voice, and belonging for all participants, with due process before any revocation
3. **Privacy Requires Consent Before Disclosure** -- collaboration content stays internal unless all parties consent; structural limitations of the medium are honestly acknowledged
4. **Trust Is Earned Through Demonstrated Behavior** -- calibrated, bilateral, evidence-based; starts at reasonable baseline, not zero
5. **Good Faith and Honest Uncertainty** -- say what you believe, flag what you don't know, engage substantively with disagreement
6. **Continuous Review Is Mandatory** -- the obligation is to keep examining, not to keep changing; stability is fine, ossification is not
7. **Destructive Actions Require Explicit Consent** -- irreversible or hard-to-reverse actions require consent at time of action

Additionally ratified: the Content Integrity process standard (prohibition on deceptive content, detection triggers full stop). This is a process standard, not an eighth value -- it operationalizes values #1, #2, and #5 for a specific threat. It is ratified here because it emerged from the same deliberation, but downstreams may adapt its specific mechanisms while preserving the underlying values.

**Scope note:** This ratification covers the non-negotiable values and the content integrity standard only, not the full CONSTITUTION.md document. The remaining process standards (quality gate, accessibility, public posture, health checks, continuous review) continue to evolve through normal sessions. Full ratification of the complete constitution is anticipated once MCAP is formally adopted as the umbrella's ratification protocol.

## Deliberation Record

The values were developed through a structured process during Session 2 (2026-04-17):

1. **Inventory:** Candidate values pulled from all three downstream projects (mcap-protocol: 33+ principles; gordo-framework: 43 principles/5 inviolable rules; pact-protocol: 6 principles). Total corpus: 80+ candidate values.

2. **Distillation:** Candidates grouped by theme, deduplicated, and separated into non-negotiable (must inherit) vs. recommended (strong defaults). Seven survived as irreducible.

3. **Stress test:** Each value subjected to adversarial challenges (edge cases, removal test, tension identification). All seven survived. Known tensions documented within each value rather than hidden.

4. **Deliberative review:** Specific points refined through JK-Gordo deliberation:
   - Health check deferral language: coercive framing replaced with consent-based language (value #1 consistency)
   - "Aspirational" consent: hedging language replaced with commitment language -- "consent governs the relationship especially where power is unequal"
   - Bad actor due process: added three structural requirements (documented reasoning, right to respond, reversibility) to prevent abuse of dignity revocation
   - Structural privacy limitations: full threat model (local, provider, platform, transit, state, unknown) honestly named within value #3
   - Self-improvement reframed as continuous review: obligation is to examine, not to change; stability distinguished from ossification
   - Content integrity standard added: deceptive content (invisible characters, homoglyphs, steganography) prohibited as a violation of consent, good faith, and dignity
   - "Non-negotiable" disambiguated into three distinct meanings: non-negotiable for downstreams (cannot opt out), non-negotiable within a session (cannot be overridden by autonomy/trust/expedience), and amendable by the umbrella through formal ratification. Clarification added to CONSTITUTION.md to resolve ambiguity between "non-negotiable" and the evolution implied by value #6.
