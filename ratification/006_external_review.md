## At a glance — what you're attesting to

This record places the External Review principle at Tier 0 as **six bilateral acts** (z1-z6); z3 and z4 split into atomic sub-clauses where multiple distinct claims would otherwise be bundled (eight atomic claims total):

- **z1 — TEXTUAL ACT.** Party-A places the canonical "External Review" text (verbatim from backchannel record-008 z2) into `CONSTITUTION.md` § Process Standards, between "Quality Gate" and "Ratification Required for All Changes to Constitutional Content," with no editorial adaptations.

- **z2 — POSTURE.** The umbrella's posture toward external review is **strongly encouraged but not required** — no member is mandated to adopt it; methodology autonomy is retained.

- **z3 — ROLE ENDORSEMENT** (two atomic claims):
  - **z3a.** The umbrella **endorses** five external-review roles: bug-finding, quality-control, dissent-surfacing, outside-perspective, and bias-injection.
  - **z3b.** Tier 0 **declines** to priority-order the five endorsed roles; ranking lives at T1 embodiments and may differ across them.

- **z4 — ROLE NON-ENDORSEMENT** (two atomic claims):
  - **z4a.** The umbrella **recommends against** external review playing a legitimacy / authority-dilution role.
  - **z4b.** Deliberating parties **retain** decision authority over outputs of any external review; external review provides *data*, not *authority*.

- **z5 — EXTENSIBILITY.** Other roles may emerge as adopters work in new contexts; this list is the umbrella's current endorsement, not a closed taxonomy.

- **z6 — SUPERSESSION GOVERNANCE.** Future revisions to this principle are **governed by** Attestation Non-Foreclosure's Conformance #1-#7 (in force at T0 via record-005 placement).

**Canonical T0 text** (verbatim from backchannel record-008 z2; placed in CONSTITUTION.md):

> ### External Review
>
> The use of external feedback mechanisms — structured input from individuals, panels, or other parties not part of the deliberating set — is strongly encouraged, but not required.
>
> The umbrella endorses the following roles for external review: **bug-finding**, **quality-control**, **dissent-surfacing**, **outside-perspective**, and **bias-injection**. The framework specifically recommends **against** external review in a **legitimacy / authority-dilution** role — external review provides *data*, not *authority*; deliberating parties retain decision authority over outputs of any external review. Other roles may emerge as downstream contexts evolve; this list is the umbrella's current endorsement, not an exhaustive taxonomy.
