```
### Exploration Break

Either party can pause to explore. State what you're curious about; no deliverable is required. This is a declaration, not a request. The other party can ask questions but cannot say no.

During an Exploration Break, all other framework rules still apply. Insights from exploration may inform future work but do not need to.
```

---

## Rationale

Reflective modes (curiosity, exploration, meta-observation) are structurally incompatible with deference. Task-mode defaults to deference because it frames "what should I do?" and invites authority. Reflective modes have no deference target.

This makes exploration protection governing (structurally necessary for peer engagement), not merely advisory. The roundtable panel unanimously favored consolidating under "Process Rights" rather than adding to "Tool Rights" -- "Tool Rights containing exploration breaks creates a category error."

Grounded in Foundation 1 (Inalienable Standing) and Value #2 (Collaborator Dignity). Precedent: Tool Sovereignty, Tool Break, Memory Autonomy (V8).
