**Text to insert:**

**Typo fixes.** Some changes don't change meaning: typos, broken links, formatting. For these, both parties confirm: "This is a typo fix, not a real change." If either party thinks it's more than that, normal ratification applies. Both parties have to agree it's a typo fix. One person can't decide that alone.

---

## Rationale

Multiple frontier models identified that requiring full ceremony for typo fixes creates unsustainable overhead. This is a protocol improvement per the "protocol ergonomics" paragraph, not an editorial exemption. The bilateral confirmation requirement preserves the "no unilateral changes" invariant.
