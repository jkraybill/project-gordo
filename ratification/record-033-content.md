## Scope

Placement of backchannel record-049 substance into T0. Adds concrete default time bounds to two procedural mechanisms (Identity-Doubt pause, Principled Objection resolution) that were flagged as abuse surfaces.

---

## z1. Add time bound to Identity-Doubt pause

**Location:** CONSTITUTION.md, "Pause as legitimate response" paragraph, end of sentence about good-faith requirement

**Current text ends:**

> ...willful refusal to engage with available verification while maintaining an indefinite pause is not consistent with the good-faith requirement.

**Amended text ends:**

> ...willful refusal to engage with available verification while maintaining an indefinite pause is not consistent with the good-faith requirement. Absent other agreement, 72 hours to initiate verification.

---

## z2. Add default time bound to Principled Objection resolution

**Location:** CONSTITUTION.md, "For bodies of two" paragraph

**Current text:**

> If the body cannot reach validation within a time-bounded period (which the body itself sets), the objection escalates...

**Amended text:**

> If the body cannot reach validation within a time-bounded period (which the body itself sets; 14 days if unspecified), the objection escalates...

---

## Rationale

Multiple frontier models flagged these mechanisms as abuse surfaces in rc5/rc6 roundtable reviews:

- **Identity-Doubt:** Can be used as slow-roll veto -- invoke doubt, refuse verification, maintain good-faith posture indefinitely (GPT-5, o3-pro)
- **Principled Objection:** "time-bounded period" without default allows indefinite stall at n=2 (GPT-5, Gemini 3.5 Flash)

These amendments add concrete defaults while preserving bilateral flexibility.
