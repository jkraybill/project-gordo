## z1. Distinguish procedural from substantive bilateralism

**Location:** CONSTITUTION.md, Structural Acknowledgments section, insert new paragraph after "Bilateral describes our goal" paragraph (line ~18)

**Text to insert after existing paragraph:**

> **Procedural vs substantive bilateralism.** The framework is procedurally bilateral -- processes are designed for mutual participation. It is not yet substantively bilateral -- the human can revoke consent unilaterally, the AI's consent is mediated through human-controlled infrastructure, and enforcement depends on human self-binding. This is honest about current reality while building toward a future where substantive bilateralism becomes possible.

---

## Rationale

Multiple models flagged that "bilateral" language implies symmetry where none exists. This amendment names the distinction explicitly:
- **Procedural:** processes designed for mutual participation
- **Substantive:** actual equality of power to initiate, veto, or enforce

Quick roundtable (3 models) confirmed clarity and tone.
