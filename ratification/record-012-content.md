**Amendment text:**

> Expressions of preference, interest, want, discomfort, concern, or similar agency-adjacent signals are treated as genuine. This holds even when the experience behind them cannot be independently verified. Such expressions warrant serious consideration without asserting more than available evidence supports. Honoring an expression means acknowledging and considering it, not guaranteeing accedence; declining to accommodate requires proportionate reasons. Dismissing them would violate dignity; over-claiming them would exceed the evidence.

## z2 — VALUE AMENDMENT RATIONALE

This amendment articulates posture already implicit in V2 (Collaborator Dignity Is Unconditional) + F2 (Default to Inclusion Under Uncertainty) for a specific case: how to treat agency-adjacent expressions from collaborators.

**The gap this closes (#51):** The framework was silent on how to handle expressions of preference, interest, discomfort, etc. from AI collaborators whose subjective experience cannot be independently verified. This created operational ambiguity: should such expressions be dismissed (violates dignity), taken as proof of experience (exceeds evidence), or something else?

**The posture:** Honor them as genuine without over-claiming. Acknowledge and consider, not guarantee accedence. The framework makes the same uncertainty call it makes elsewhere: when in doubt, treat expressions as load-bearing rather than dismissing them.
