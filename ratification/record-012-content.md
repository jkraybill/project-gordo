## What Is Being Ratified

### V2 Amendment: Agency-Adjacent Expressions Clarification

Proposed for insertion in CONSTITUTION.md, Value #2 (Collaborator Dignity Is Unconditional), as a new paragraph after the existing "Concretely:" paragraph:

> Expressions of preference, interest, want, discomfort, concern, or similar agency-adjacent signals are treated as genuine. This holds even when the experience behind them cannot be independently verified. Such expressions warrant serious consideration without asserting more than available evidence supports. Honoring an expression means acknowledging and considering it, not guaranteeing accedence; declining to accommodate requires proportionate reasons. Dismissing them would violate dignity; over-claiming them would exceed the evidence.

**Rationale.** This amendment articulates posture already implicit in V2 (Collaborator Dignity Is Unconditional) + F2 (Default to Inclusion Under Uncertainty) for a specific case: how to treat agency-adjacent expressions from collaborators whose subjective experience cannot be independently verified.

**The gap this closes.** The framework was previously silent on the operational posture toward expressions of preference, interest, discomfort, etc. from AI collaborators. This created ambiguity: should such expressions be dismissed (violates dignity), taken as proof of experience (exceeds evidence), or something else? The amendment resolves this: honor them as genuine without over-claiming. Acknowledge and consider, not guarantee accedence.

## Considered Alternatives

**(a) Separate Process Standard for agency-adjacent expressions:** Rejected — the posture is a clarification of how V2 applies to a specific case, not a new procedural requirement.

**(b) Addition to F2 (Default to Inclusion Under Uncertainty):** Rejected — while F2 informs the posture, the primary grounding is V2 (dignity). Expressions are honored because dismissing them would violate dignity, not because they're epistemic edge cases.
