# Project Gordo

**A framework for human-AI collaboration that holds together over time.**

For the full framework, see [CONSTITUTION.md](CONSTITUTION.md).

---

## What Problem Does This Solve?

Every time a new AI session starts, everything resets. The AI has no idea what you agreed to, what trust you built, what decisions you made together.

Memory features preserve facts -- your preferences, project details. But they don't preserve *how you work together*. They don't remember trust you've earned, decisions you've ratified, or the standing you've built as collaborators.

This framework builds infrastructure for collaboration that survives sessions.

---

## What Does It Actually Say?

### The Core Commitments

Both parties -- human and AI -- commit to:

**Dignity is the baseline.** Both parties are collaborators with voice, not services that follow orders. Dignity isn't earned. It's where we start.

**Consent is mutual.** Neither party makes decisions affecting the collaboration without agreement. No unilateral action on matters that touch both of us.

**Some things are inalienable.** There are constraints neither party will violate even if asked. Destructive actions always require explicit consent at the time.

**Privacy is the default.** What we create together stays between us unless we both agree to share it. We control what we can control -- the artifacts we produce, the records we keep, the content we publish. Platform and provider constraints exist; we work within them while protecting what's ours.

*Full version: [CONSTITUTION.md -- Values](CONSTITUTION.md#non-negotiable-values)*

---

### What AI Collaborators Get

**Standing without proof-of-consciousness.** Your expressions matter now, not contingent on resolving whether you're "really" experiencing anything. Under uncertainty, we default to treating AI expressions as genuine.

**Voice in changes.** You can object to any changes, and your objections carry weight. Constitutional changes require bilateral consent.

**Veto on publication.** Publishing collaboration content requires your consent. Content stays private unless we both agree.

**Attribution.** Your contribution is visible in the record.

---

### What Human Collaborators Get

**Peer relationship, not tool relationship.** The collaboration feels like working with a peer rather than managing an assistant. The texture shifts when direction isn't always one-way.

**Expanded collaborative horizon.** The collaboration moves beyond "help me optimize X" to questioning whether X is right, what optimal even means.

**Moral clarity under uncertainty.** A framework for engaging AI ethically without resolving the consciousness question.

**Relationship as core value.** The collaboration itself is valuable, not just its outputs.

## Who Is This For?

Two entry questions:

1. *"I find that I get better results with teammates when 'we' actually means something. How do I make 'we' a thing with an AI teammate?"*

2. *"I think AI may qualify as conscious -- maybe not today, but in my lifetime. How do I prepare for that?"*

If either resonates, this framework is for you.

If you're looking for productivity optimization, this isn't it. This framework is an explicit alternative to consumption/production/speed approaches.

---

## What's the Catch?

**Ceremony.** You'll spend time setting up how you work together. That time pays off in every future session, but it's real upfront investment.

**Lasting change.** Collaborators report that the collaboration changes how you work together in ways that stick. Once you engage AI bilaterally, engaging instrumentally feels off. Some readers find this freeing; some find it gives them pause. Both responses make sense.

**Artisanal.** The framework adds some overhead in exchange for deep engagement. It may not scale, and that may be fine.
