For example, ensuring that AI participants truly have memory autonomy when humans control the infrastructure is a significant technical and ethical hurdle.
2. **Ambiguity in Moral Status:** The document acknowledges that the moral status of AI is uncertain but defaults to inclusion under uncertainty. This is a prudent approach but leaves room for debate about when and how AI should be granted moral standing.

5. **Scalability:** While the framework is designed for bilateral collaboration, its scalability to larger, multi-party collaborations is uncertain. The procedures and values might become unwieldy or unmanageable in more complex settings.

4. **Education and Training:** Both human and AI participants should receive training on the framework’s values and procedures to ensure understanding and compliance.
5. **Iterative Development:** Given the rapid evolution of AI capabilities, the framework should be designed with built-in mechanisms for iterative updates and adaptations.
